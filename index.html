Battlebits Manual

What is Battlebits?

Battlebits is a stream RPG battling minigame on Twitch.

Currently in alpha. This document will change as development progresses.
Follow @BERDGames on Twitter for development updates once Battlebits hits beta!

What is all this on the screen?

  1. Name
  2. Health
  3. Power
  4. Rank
  5. Job

Put me in the ring, coach!

To join the battle, you can either

  • Cheer any amount of bits
  • Subscribe or resubscribe to the channel
  • Following the channel

Cheering

When you cheer, you can do one of two things; join the battle or support a combatant.

Fighting

To join the battle, you don't have to do anything special, just cheer away!
The amount of bits you cheer when you join the battle equals the amount of health you'll start with.

Additionally, you can pick a job to grant yourself extra perks while fighting.
To pick a job, you just add the corresponding hashtag in your message. Check the Jobs heading below to see which jobs you can pick.

If a battle is already in progress, you will be put in the queue. Otherwise, you will be put in the empty spot.

If you're already battling, you will add power to yourself instead. You do not need to add #left or #right to add power yourself.
You can also change jobs if you want. Just add the new job's hashtag in the message and you'll switch jobs.

Supporting

To support a person on the battlefield, you can cheer by putting either #left or #right in your message.
This will give the person you cheer on power. The amount of bits corresponds to how much power you'll give them.
If you want to heal the person, see the Healing header below.

NOTE: If there is no person in that spot, you will enter that spot as a combatant.

Rank

When you join as a combatant, you will be given a rank depending on how much you cheered.
You can also advance in rank if you heal yourself or if someone supports you to get over the thresholds.
Rank does not influence the battle in any way, it is purely cosmetic.

The ranks are as follows:

  • 1: 1 - 99
  • 2: 100 - 999
  • 3: 1000 - 4999
  • 4: 5000 - 9999
  • 5: 10000 and above

Subscribing

When you subscribe or resubscribe to the channel, you will be entered as a combatant with 500 health, as if you cheered 500 bits.
You will get a randomly assigned job.

Following

When you follow the channel, you will be entered as a combatant with 10 health, as if you cheered 10 bits. You cannot pick a job.

NOTE: Following is only counted once. Unfollowing and re-following will not count.

The Fight

Each fight consists of quick turns. The battle continues until one or both combatants reach 0 HP.
One turn consists of either an attack or a heal (depending on the job). Attacks can miss.

Power

During the fight, you'll accumulate power over time or when you or someone else cheers for you.

Power has two uses right now: Limit Break and healing.

Healing (#heal or #powerHeal)

If you want to heal instead of adding power, add #heal to the message. This will use all the bits in the message to heal instead.
If you are fighting, you can tap into power to heal by using #powerHeal. This will use up all the bits in the message and all of your power to heal yourself.

If you don't want to use all of the bits and/or power, you can specify an amount to #heal>amount< and #powerHeal>amount<.
For example, if you cheer with 500 bits and use #heal200, you'll get 200 HP and get 300 power.
If you're fighting, cheer using 300 bits and use #powerHeal200, you'll get 200 HP and get 100 power.
If you instead use #powerHeal400, you'll use all 300 bits and 100 of your power to heal for 400 HP.

Limit Break (#lb)

Cost: 500 power
If you have 500 power, you can use a Limit Break to deal 1000 damage by adding #lb to your cheer message.
Bits are also counted to the power cost, so you can have 450 power and cheer 50 bits and activate the Limit Break.
Only the person fighting can initiate a Limit Break.

Jobs

There are four jobs in Battlebits

  • Freelancer
  • Paladin
  • White Mage
  • Ninja

Each job, aside from Freelancer, has traits and abilities that occur troughout the battle.
Freelancer is the default job, and as such has no special abilities or traits.

Traits are passive effects unique to each job.

Abilities are time-activated effects. You cannot decide when to activate them, they will be activated during the course of the battle.

Paladin (#pld)

Trait: Block
Has a chance to halve the damage taken.

Ability: Stun
The Paladin stuns the opponent for a short period of time.
While active, the opponent cannot attack, heal or use abilities.

White Mage (#whm)

Trait: Healing
Has a chance to heal themselves instead of dealing damage.

Ability: Regen
The White Mage casts Regen, causing them to continously heal.
While active, in addition to the damage or healing done that turn, an additional flat value heal is performed.

Ninja (#nin)

Trait: Crit
Has a chance to deal double damage.

Ability: Huton
The Ninja speeds up their attacks.
While active, Ninja attacks twice in one turn.
